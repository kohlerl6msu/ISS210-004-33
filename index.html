About

The issue of legalizing marijuana is complicated within the United States. With it becoming a racial/social issue with “Black people 3.64 times more likely than white people to be arrested for marijuana possession, notwithstanding comparable usage rates” (via ACLU.org). In my own life, I’ve heard of a story of a white male who was around 17 years old. He got pulled over by the police with a substantial amount of marijuana in his trunk, but the person’s father knew the cop personally. With this connection, he was able to keep driving and be exempt from any trouble that many others have faced. What if it was a poor African American male driving through a rich suburb, would the consequences be similar? There is a long history of the prohibition of marijuana, and it mainly stems from racist origins. Throughout our website, we talk about the positives of the legalization of marijuana, the history of marijuana, and statistics regarding marijuana. We believe that marijuana should be legal, while it should be used in moderation—it poses many benefits. With our history trying to impose in us that marijuana is bad, it evidently poses more benefits for many people. If you want to help this cause and/or be more informed: visit norml.org.

Video Title

To summarize the video in short: When Mexicans immigrated to the United States in the 1900s they brought along smoking marijuana (or marihuana) recreationally. Right after the prohibition ended in 1933—Harry Anslinger wanted to put an end to marijuana. He took the non-evident claim that marijuana was a violence inducing drug and connected that to African American and Hispanic people to cause terror and cause more discrimination with the Marihuana Tax Act of 1937. Alongside this act, Harry Anslinger claimed marijuana made black people “forget their place in society” and took a shot at the predominantly African-American genre of music: Jazz. He claimed that jazz was “evil music” and was formed under the influence of marijuana. After the rebellious hippy era of the 60s—Nixon repealed the Marihuana Tax Act in 1970. Instead, he reclassified it as a schedule 1 drug; the schedule 1 drug is classified as a high potential for abuse and addiction (other drugs in that category: heroin, LSD, and ecstasy). In a report in 2010, it was said that both black and white people consume marijuana at the same rate, but black people have been approximately four times more likely to be arrested for marijuana possession. In society today, using Marijuana recreationally is legal in some states, but the federal law still stands that marijuana is a schedule 1 controlled substance.

Title of Image 4

Over the years, public opinion on legalizing weed has undergone a significant shift. The Pew Research Center's article "Public Opinion about Legalizing Marijuana" covers this change. According to the article, while support for legalizing marijuana has remained relatively stable in recent years, there has been a significant increase in support for legalization over the past few decades. In the 1990s, only about 25% of Americans supported legalizing marijuana, but by 2021, that number had increased to 60%. This change in public opinion can be attributed to several factors, including increased awareness of the medical benefits of marijuana, the failure of the war on drugs, and a growing recognition of racial disparities in drug enforcement. As a result, more and more states and countries are legalizing marijuana for medical and recreational use, and the stigma surrounding marijuana use is slowly diminishing.

Title of Image 5

Marijuana has been a controversial topic for decades, with debates ranging from its legalisation to its potential harms on individuals. However, recent studies have shed light on the potential benefits of marijuana use for specific groups of people, including teenagers. In this essay, I will discuss the mental health and pain management benefits that marijuana can offer to teens, acknowledge any potential risks associated with teenage marijuana use and how they can be minimised through responsible usage and regulation. Marijuana has been shown to potentially improve mental health conditions such as anxiety and depression in teens. A study conducted by Caleb J. Pawl et al. (2022) found correlations between cannabis use and decreased levels of depression symptoms among Michigan residents after the state's legalisation of recreational cannabis use. Additionally, Elliot C. Wallace et al.'s (2022) research highlights that medicinal uses of marijuana may help those suffering from PTSD-induced symptoms as well. Marijuana has also been linked to alleviating chronic pain in teens caused by illnesses or injuries. Studies show that THC, a compound found in marijuana plants, may play a role in decreasing inflammation leading to chronic pain relief (Xiong et al., 2012). Furthermore, some researchers suggest using medical cannabis as an alternative treatment option for managing neuropathic pain related to different neurological disorders(Panaligan & Valenzuela, 2018). As with any substance used recreationally or medicinally there are risks associated with it; however some concerns about teen consumption need more immediate attention than others like impaired cognitive function or addiction issues.These risks can be reduced significantly through regulations that ensure safe consumption practices which include providing parental education along with age verification protocols designed at dispensary level rather than criminalising its use altogether. In conclusion, marijuana could provide various therapeutic benefits when utilised properly. Based on the research I have discussed, marijuana has the potential to help teens with mental health conditions such as anxiety and depression. Additionally it can be used for pain management in teenagers who suffer from chronic illnesses or injuries. While there are certainly risks associated with teenage marijuana use, they could be minimised through responsible usage and regulation practices that ensure safe consumption practices at all times.Marijuana has been shown to have a positive impact on individuals when used properly, and it is important to continue further research in this area.
